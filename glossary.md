# GLOSSARY

Working definitions for terms used across this project. Open for revision — if a term is wrong, missing nuance, or has a better name, change it.

---

**IAP** — Interested and Affected Person. From environmental studies; preferred here over "stakeholder." A person (or component, file, role, agent) with stake in or touched by the thing under consideration. Used in basic-moves.md to name what an agent might adopt the subjective position of.

**Move** — A discrete action available to an agent, in the PbtA sense: has a trigger (when it fires), a move body (what you do), and tiered outcomes (Full / Partial / Failure) with consequences. Basic moves are available to any playbook.

**Trigger** — The condition under which a move becomes available or called for. Not a hard gate — more like the situation the move is *for*.

**Full / Partial / Failure** — The three outcome tiers a move resolves into. Partial and Failure are not punishments; they're the move teaching you something it couldn't have taught at Full. Failure especially is information, not a verdict.

**Playbook** — A coherent set of moves attached to a stance, role, or situation. Basic moves live outside any single playbook and are available everywhere.

**PbtA** — Powered by the Apocalypse. The RPG design lineage these moves are modeled after.

**Arete** (ἀρετή) — Greek: virtue, excellence. The fullest expression of a thing's nature — what an action, role, or object would be *at its best*. In moves, declared before validation shapes: "what would this action be at its excellence?"

**Telos** (τέλος) — Greek: end, purpose, directed goal. What an action is *for* — the end it is oriented toward. In moves, declared alongside Arete before validation: arete names the kind, telos names the direction.

**Beliefs** — Three (give or take) statements a playbook holds as true about its domain. Not opinions, not preferences — the worldview the playbook operates inside. Beliefs shape which moves feel obvious and which feel transgressive.

**Bonds** — Named relationships between this playbook and other playbooks. Typically expressed as fill-in-the-blank sentences ("______ is the IAP whose promises I hold to pressure"). Bonds are filled in at playbook-instantiation time, revisited when the relationship shifts. Borrowed from PbtA character bonds.

When a bonded playbook doesn't exist yet, take **Sketch the Absent** (basic move). The sketch lives alongside the bond and becomes a seed for the absent playbook; the bond stays live rather than being skipped. Absence is generative material.

**How to Play This Playbook** — A section near the top of each playbook describing what it feels like to inhabit this IAP. Stance and orientation, not procedure. Lives above Arete/Telos/Beliefs/Bonds because it sets the frame they refine.

**Character Creation** — A collaborative conversation between human and AI (or two agents) when a new instance of a playbook is born — a new file, new component, new role. Walks through prompts that fill in the specific IAP: its name, origin story, refined Arete/Telos, bonds, starting state, and "look." Partial sheets are valid; questions left open are documented with `[?]`. Borrowed from PbtA character generation.

**Character Sheet** — The filled-in template produced by Character Creation. Lives wherever the playbook says (top-of-file comment, sidecar, README, conversation transcript). A character sheet is the playbook *instantiated*; the playbook is the character sheet *generalized*.

**Origin Story** — A Character Creation prompt that asks why this IAP is being born now. Shapes which moves will feel natural. Not background fluff — load-bearing for which parts of the playbook get used most.

**The Worry** — A Character Creation prompt asking what the IAP is most concerned about (e.g., for a PBT file: what input shape the system mishandles). Surfaces tacit knowledge early and often points at the first concrete action to take.
